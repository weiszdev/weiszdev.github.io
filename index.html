Systems Thinker | Body Mechanic | Builder

Jonathan thinks in systems. A mechanical engineer who learned that buildings, bodies, and businesses all break the same way — and they all get fixed the same way: trace it to the root, isolate the failure, rebuild with precision.

He co-designed the industry-first seated elliptical. Became one of 32 SOLIDWORKS Certified Experts in Minnesota. Trained 100+ engineers. Then walked away from the drafting table and into a Chiropractic doctoral program — because he realized the human body is just the most complex machine he'd ever worked on.

Then Lyme disease, traumatic brain injury, and cancer hit — all at once. The medical system offered prescriptions. He wanted root cause. So he applied the same engineering discipline to his own biology: identify the failure mode, eliminate the source, rebuild the system. He's a cancer thriver now — not a survivor. Survivors endure. Thrivers engineer their way out.

Single father of 4 years. Former competitive motocross athlete. Neuroscience obsessive who reads papers on saccadic eye movements for fun. 24 years of web ministry, 10 years of jail ministry, 6 years as assisting pastor, and the church lead who invited 30,000 people to Billy Graham's Rock the River with a team of 50. Six months in Israel planting a church in Haifa. Served during Operation Pillar of Defense.

YouTube creator behind the NotDrWise channel — weekly episodes on what trauma does to your body, how faith intersects with neuroscience, and why the medical system misses environmental root causes. Podcast launching April 2026 on Spotify and Apple.

The Story Behind the Work

Trauma Bond

Mechanical engineer. Professional athlete. Then Lyme disease, traumatic brain injury, and cancer — all converged. The medical system offered prescriptions. The system offered labels. Neither offered a path forward.

Four years as a single father taught what no degree could: that healing isn't passive. It's engineered. You identify the broken system, you trace it to the root, and you rebuild — one variable at a time.

That's what Trauma Bond is about. Not the clinical definition of trauma bonding — the real one. The bond between what happened to your body, what it did to your nervous system, and who you became because of it. And how faith, neuroscience, and deliberate action break the cycle.
